Our Services:

We provide services in 2 program areas: Skill Instruction and Community Engagement.

Skill Instruction

  • Self Awareness

    Build participant knowledge and awareness of their abilities and differences, using a strengths-based approach. Instruction will focus on developing skills to manage their challenges and advocate for their needs.

  • Self Discovery for Jobs

    Help participants explore their aptitudes and interests with a combination of traditional skill assessment tools and strategies adapted from the Discovering Personal Genius program.

  • Job Seeking

    Build concrete skills to help autistic youth find and retain jobs. Skill instruction includes how to prepare for and engage in job interviews, how to handle online applications and how to communicate in professional networks.

  • Post-Secondary Navigation

    Help students develop executive function and communication skills in post-secondary environments to support their successful completion of selected degree programs.

  • Skill Practice

    Provide supported opportunities to engage in community and job events to develop community connections and practice communication skills in real-world settings. Example engagement opportunities includes attendance at job fairs and conducting information interviews with community employers


Community Engagement

  • “On the Spectrum” Series

    Conduct community education sessions for college staff, faculty and employers. The goal of this service is to build the knowledge and insights of individuals who are working with autistic individuals in classrooms and the workplace. The sessions provide information about autistic thinking styles and offer practical strategies to promote student and employee success.

  • Job Coaching

    Provide job coaching advice and supports for autistic employers and employees, with the goal of supporting workplace success for autistic employees and provide continued learning opportunities about autism for employers.
