Our Services:

We provide services in 3 program areas: Skill Instruction, Community Education, and College Support

What you will learn:

  • Strategies to answer common interview questions
  • How to highlight your skills
  • How to manage tricky situations like job loss and issues with eye contact

Skill Instruction

  • Self Awareness

    Build participant knowledge and awareness of their abilities and differences, using a strengths-based approach. Instruction will focus on developing skills to manage their challenges and advocate for their needs.

  • Self Discovery for Jobs

    Help participants explore their aptitudes and interests with a combination of traditional skill assessment tools and strategies adapted from the Discovering Personal Genius program.

  • Job Preparedness

    Build concrete skills to help autistic youth find and retain jobs. Skill instruction includes how to prepare for and engage in job interviews, how to handle online applications and how to communicate in professional networks.

  • Post-Secondary Navigation

    Help students develop executive function and communication skills in post-secondary environments to support their successful completion of selected degree programs.

  • Skill Practice

    Provide supported opportunities to engage in community and job events to develop community connections and practice communication skills in real-world settings. Example engagement opportunities includes attendance at job fairs and conducting information interviews with community employers

Community Education

  • “On the Spectrum” Series

    Conduct community education sessions and webinars for college staff, faculty and employers. The goal of this service is to build the knowledge and insights of individuals who are working with autistic individuals in classrooms and the workplace. The sessions provide information about autistic thinking styles and offer practical strategies to promote student and employee success.

  • Job Coaching

    Provide job coaching advice and supports for autistic employers and employees, with the goal of supporting workplace success for autistic employees and provide continued learning opportunities about autism for employers.

College Support

Many young people with higher functioning autism spectrum go to college after high school. Since they often do well academically in a K-12 setting, it is generally assumed that they’ll manage well in college and beyond. But they continue to have Autism and they continue to need the structure and supports they were provided while growing up.

Conventional wisdom holds that the biggest problems for higher functioning individuals are social and communication issues. But they also struggle with life skills and disrupted executive function —the mental processes involving organization, planning, time management and multitasking. This makes college, their first venture into independent living, especially challenging. Their executive functioning capabilities are swamped by the new responsibilities of managing an independent life as a college student -- living in a dorm or apartment, scheduling homework and maintaining proper nutrition.

These new challenges occur at the same time they lose the educational support services they’ve had through most of their life. Many students also have other, accompanying problems, including anxiety and ADHD, that may makes self-management much harder.
